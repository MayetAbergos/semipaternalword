"Praise be to the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, the Father of compassion and the God of all comfort, who comforts us in all our troubles, so that we can comfort those in any trouble with the comfort we ourselves receive from God." 2 Cor 1:3-4

April-August, 2018. What better to spend it? Rest, bond with the family, make time for friends, enjoy and relax. However, God had other plans I guess? Thank you so much Merlyn Tabo, God had used you for His purpose in my life.

CHAPTER I

Sometime this year I learned how important it is to seek God's Word before making any decisions and align our plans with His. He only wants the best and His plans are far greater than ours.

I want to share how God answers when we communicate with Him, through His Word, the Bible; He answers through parables that only you, can understand. He uses people as well, to lead you where you should go, what you should and exactly what you need to hear when you need it. It is more like a communication with your friend. We pray to speak with God, he answers back through His Word through what we call "Devotion" You can ask me more about it, if you want to.

I prayed about the job offered and here's what God said,

And, I took that as a yes.

Monday, April 16, 2018. We went to the recruitment site at Eton Cyberpod in front of Robinson's Galleria, Ortigas. Believe me, I had no idea what was going to happen, I was terrified. I recently graduated from high school, have no job experience, had never been to real job interviews or whatsoever. All I know was how to make a resume, and oh, sit erect when being interviewed. But, God said yes, so I claimed that everything will run smoothly and fine. Why was I worrying in the first? God's got this.

To cut the long story short we went through a lot of procedures, and praise God, got hired. Contract signing was around 12 midnight April 17, 2018, yes, it took as one whole day, but it was all worth it. When He says, He fulfills.

In the next few days, Gabrielle Ramos (my high school cherished friend and my Jollibee C2 buddy) and I finished up all the requirements, SSS, Pag-ibig, NBI, medical check-ups etc. What made it more fun is that one of my dearest friends, Patricia Joson applied and got hired too!

Photo credits: Gabrielle Ramos

There were 22 of us and 17 people I did not know. People of different ages with different life experiences, principles and God wanted to reach out to them. As soon as my supervisor Mae Carandang-Panlilio introduced herself, I knew that this was from God, it was a God-given opportunity that needs to put action upon.

She was this cool, fun and millenial-like supervisor who does not like being called "Ma'am" or "Boss" and, did I mention that she loves dogs? Yes! Right time, in the right place with the right people. Thank You! I was thrilled.

Monday, April 23, 2018. First day on the job, everything was new. I was excited and nervous. But hey! Money and experience set aside, I believed in my heart that God was definitely up to something.

-End-

CHAPTER II

Photo Taken: This is Universal 28. They were the people I was with during training.

We had exactly, 40 days of training. Work never felt like "work" though, because the atmosphere was light and it felt more like we were attending special classes for the summer and we were there to learn how to engage with people we never met, hear voices we have to listen to, and be of help to them as if they were not on the other side of the earth. It was nice.

As training went by, I got to know them and things got serious. Maintaining the necessary metrics, like grades in school is one thing but it is another thing to carry a lot of weight of the world to work and act like everything is going well. As I spoke to each person in that class, I can't help but thank God for what He has done and His love. People need to be reminded of how much God loves them because the world will make you forget. It is going to distract you, hurt you, break you, and lose your sight of His purpose for your life. We will constantly illuminate pain, struggle, low-esteem, shame and fear.

Photo: Universal 28

God did His best, to use me as His light to lift others up. There, I was opened to the real world. The life outside school. During the training, in God's name I shared the wonders of having that personal relationship with God. The feeling of being held up high when everything seems to be falling apart. His Word, His gospel, His comfort is what stirs people to live every day and be strong in the midst of dark seasons.

I admire a lot of people at work. They strive and work at their best to put plate on the table. Everyone I met, taught me a lesson that I will carry for life. In the same way that I hope that I was able to remind them that it is not about religion, it not about how good we are, is about how good He is and what He demonstrated on that cross to make us whole. That every time you see that cross, I pray that you will be reminded that you are, valued, you have a purpose and you are, loved.

Photo: Training days

If you are reading this, right now. God is speaking to you. Kaya mo daw yan :)

-End-

CHAPTER III

Days past, and I enjoyed meeting more and more people. I felt bliss and participated in various activities and won prizes as a team! I thank, my coaches and mentors who helped me not just as an employee but as person. Thank you for the life stories you shared. I will keep them safe with me and I will always, always pray for all of you.

Photo: Team Kenn First team building activity for the 4th of July Celebration

Photo: Last team building before I left for College

Beyond the names and accounts I opened, I hope that I became of good assistance to the people I answered the call and a good colleague. Working at a call center company is not easy, you work at night, sleep in the morning. More time for work, have less time for yourself, family and friends. Stress and traffic is there. You will get burned out. Plus, you need a lot of patience, respect, open-mindedness, love, care, empathy, understanding, genuineness, and peace in your heart.

I will never forget that line that Ms. Zuniga (my former teacher in English in high school) said during our class, "Everyone is fighting battles you do not know about, so always be kind" and, indeed it is true. I applied here in my job and let it manifest.

Photo Taken: Lunch at Tagaytay

I am no longer going to put into detail all that transpired during those 4 months, but every moment has become a big part of who I am now. It made me realize that life could be tough without God. I became more understanding and open to listening to others. I was one of the youngest employees and just imagine having a lot of ates and kuyas surrounding you. It could change you, big time.

Photo Taken: Team lunch at 3:00 am in the morning

Talking to complete strangers from hundreds and miles away was kinda fun, being around with people I knew for a few months and listening to how they gone through life was an extraordinary experience. During my last day, I made the most out of it and gave out letters to each person I knew to show gratitude and appreciation. It was heartbreaking saying goodbye but I had to.

Photo: Team Kenn after winning the decorate your coach's station activity

Photo: Universal 28 during lunch break

I became part of a big family of Dow Jones, Sitel. It was an honor and privilege. Thank you, U.S.A.'s leading newspaper, the Wall Street Journal. My purpose is done, it is time to go on and move forward to another chapter God wrote.
