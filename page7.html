CHAPTER I

Every time people ask what course I'm taking their usual responses are;

"Huh?"

"What?"

It's no surprise at all. Honestly, even I myself knew much less about it. Growing up we all have our dreams and aspirations; as a child I didn't quite dreamed of becoming a Speech-Language Pathologist (SLP). So let's dive into defining what an SLP is first to give you a better picture as you read along the way.

According to American Speech-Language-Hearing Association, a speech-language pathologist is a health-allied course specialized for treating "Speech disorders", this is when a person is unable to produce speech sounds correctly or fluently, or has problems with their voice or resonance. Language disorders is when a person has trouble understanding others (receptive language), or sharing thoughts, ideas, and feelings (expressive language)."

Congratulations, reader! You now know a teeny-tiny information on what a speech-language pathologist is. You will understand more as you read...

Most SLPs, only found out that the course existed because of others; by others it could be a relative, a friend or a professional related to the field telling us about the profession. Fortunately enough for us, like me, there were people like them who directed us to a path that we never envisioned of taking.

What once was just a risk taken for a shallow purpose of merely having a course in college paved the way to the sensation of finding a home where hope and love resides. As my freshman year nearly comes to its end, I already have fallen deeply in love with Speech-language Pathology.

It is ever fulfilling to go through subjects wherein you learned about yourself; how we think of ourselves mattered as much as how we treat other people especially our patients. It's more than just knowledge, memorizing, deadlines and passing. It is being accountable to the lives of those who need your help; seeing them as a reflection of who we are. It's more than just positive thinking; it is giving a person something to believe in; that they are capable, loved and valued despite being in a state wherein all their life they view themselves as different, an outcast and a burden.

SLP is a life-giving profession.

Looking ahead I will always, always be grateful to all the opportunities that were opened for me. Little did I know that a simple conversation and encounter with a person shifted the entirety of the journey I was yet to walk right in.

CHAPTER II

I want to be a voice to the voiceless. Hence, I must remain strong and driven by passion to stay on track. The sleepless nights, stressful days and draining afternoons will soon pay-off. After all, it's for the benefit of many. But,

Model: Mangoba, Keziah Photo Taken by: Angus, Eunice

It's true when they say that college is not a piece of cake especially now that I am in it. I guarantee and testify 100% that it is no fun and games, not rainbows and butterflies. There are those days when I feel like giving up. Even to the point of saying,

"Ayoko na pagod na ko"

"Tutuloy ko pa ba 'to?"

All the endless long readings, quizzes almost every week, requirements to accomplish, grades to be maintained. I tell you it is draining. I rest for one whole night but still feel tired. I lie down in bed and stare, joggling problems from different aspects of life with fear crippling here and there. Anxiety kicking in, all the thoughts of failing, not being good enough, and people sucking out life in you aaaaah it's hard.

How can I fight for those in need when I am facing my own battles? Help suddenly becomes a big and heavy word when you yourself is drowning.

Have you ever wondered how people perceive the phrase "worth it"?

As for me, you to have an ideology of what is contentment and humility. It should satisfy your hunger for genuine happiness 'cause when greed and pride comes in, it will automatically blind us in serving people the way they should be served. Service comes hand-in-hand with the word empathy; it is putting ourselves in the shoes of others, it is going outside of our own comfort zones because our patients are people who strongly desire to be better; making them feel that they belong, that they can be accepted. That WE, can improve their quality of life.

They have placed their trust in us which is why money should come second, in my credence, a future profession must be something that will not only grant assistance for others but also, develop our sense of being humane thus making it a crucial part of our lives. I know it will be difficult, but it will definitely be worth it...

CHAPTER III

Inspiration came from the Latin word inspiratus, which means "inhaling, breathing in; inspiration" or in simpler terms, it is the act of getting air inside the lungs.

However, the inspiration that I would be talking about are people. I know life can be cruel it seems to hit us right in the spot most of the times and beat us up but people, people keep us going. During hardships, most of us would have some sort of escape that we create to divert what we feel. It could be talking to someone, going away for a while, reading a book, writing, playing games, or watching movies.

But you know what,

Someday, I want to sit down, let the breeze of fresh air pass through my skin with eyes closed and reminisce memories I've made throughout my life. The good ones make smile, the bad ones hmm, well we learn and grow from those. But the point is, it wouldn't be possible of course, without those who stood by no matter what.

Photo Taken by: Angus, Eunice

For each decision and step we make, we become closer to reaching our goal. Picturing the future a few years from now gets me so excited. Seeing those children— pure, sweet, innocent, kind children, just thinking about it makes me squeal! Gee, aaah! Hahaha! There's something about those little ones and I can't seem to explain. On the contrary, it would be painful to witness them having a hard time coping with the situation they did not choose to be in.

"My sister is speech delayed and has been going through sessions since she was 3 years old." — Marcus Bernardo

My brother, Jeanneluc Theodore D. Angus has Down Syndrome. He is the reason why I took the course." — Eunice Angus

These statements sound light until you realize that it is what propels a person to do things passionately...

Not often do we ask the "whys" and "hows" and that is what makes speech-language pathology so special because it seeks to understand what a person does not say. It goes back to the very roots from how the person was raised to the core cause of the anomaly. Its scope spreads from scientific approach to basic observation in our daily lives. Hearing practitioners tell about their stories inspired me to go for it and test my maximum potentials.

Another thing is that it is a combination of various fields; medicine, psychology, communication etc. We are counselors with high-spirits and warriors who restore lives. We speak words of power to encourage, motivate, and bring life. We are committed to changing the world, one word at a time. Language is a system of symbols used to communicate; communication is essential in life; life is beautiful.

We may have different reasons on why we took the course, what matters is that we finish it with the right motive pursuing only one ambition and that, is to be of service.

"Hindi mo man masabi na kailangan mo ng tulong, tutulong at tutulong kami."

What an SLP does is more than fluency, speech production, language, cognition, voice, resonance, feeding and swallowing and auditory rehabilitation. It is demonstrating life and opening doorways of possibilities for a person with disability. Patients aren't just patients. It's like having one big extended family out there.

By the time I finally achieve that dream I will be grateful to one of the institutions where I'm from; De La Salle Medical and Health Sciences Institute.

With God by our side all the frustration, pressure, failures, and wounds will fade away. He brought us here because He will use us for a greater purpose. Alleviating pain is one thing, touching lives is another. Everybody deserves to know that they are important regardless of capabilities.

Truly, I'm honored to be part of something bigger than myself. I do not in any way regret being here, taking this course, and seeing myself progress through the good and bad days.

I didn't imagine this but now I am in awe of how everything is falling into place. One person, one conversation followed by a series of turn of events and it is my turn now.

I'm looking forward to what lies ahead. I have high hopes for each and everyone. Step-up reach out with pure hearts, our life is valuable, make it count and— count others in.

Wherever life takes us,

Once an SLP 1-2, will always and forever be an SLP 1-2.
