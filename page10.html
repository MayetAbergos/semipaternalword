An Open Book

ETHEREAL

Photo: Batad-Banaue, Mountain Province

"The Lord is my strength and my shield; in Him my heart trusts." Psalm 28:7 

What better way to celebrate your transition from a teenager to an adult than being with the Lord? Like Jesus, going up the mountains to pray is ever satisfying for both the heart and the sole (pun intended).

CHAPTER I

Where do broken hearts go? That's right, Sagada. 10 hours of travel from Cubao-Coda Lines, 650 php plus 300 php to get there was definitely worth it; from the place itself to the people in it. And so, I wondered off to see more of it for myself, like the most famous "Hanging Coffins".

The adventure started and I used the amazing Google Maps.

Took a 23-min walk from Sagada's main town to the Hanging Coffins; as I was going there I met a friend. You know what they say "If you want to go fast, walk alone, if you want to go far, walk together." Besides, I am indeed, a cynophilist, lover of dogs which is why I felt blessed with its company.

Little did I know that this dog had a purpose. We continued on to walk when I noticed that it was acting strange. It kept on jumping and jumping at me as if it was trying to tell me something. So stopped, and a jeepney passed by and I asked,

E: "Kuya dito po ba papuntang Hanging Coffins?" and I was surprised by his answer.

Kuya: "Walang daan dyan."

Goosebumps.

E: "Hala saan po?"

Kuya: "Punta ka doon sa bayan tapos magtanong ka saan yung papasok."

I looked at the dog and I immediately thanked God. Imagine wasting all your time and energy feeling excited about getting to nowhere? It's like climbing up a ladder just to find out that you're on the wrong wall ctto. If it was not for that dog, who knows where that road leads to?

So,

Travelling tip #1 Do not use Google Maps on mountains. It only tracks roads. I suggest you guys go to the information center and ask for a tour guide.

Bottomline is,

Are you lost? Something or Someone is willing to take the lead and bring you back to the right path.

Like what this dog did,

and Jesus, too.

-End-

CHAPTER II

After passing by Sagada and witnessing some of the coffins in Lumiang Cave (30-45 mins walk down the mountain from the main town plus 40 mins down to the cave. Fee: 200 php, 40 php environmental fee ) I headed to Banaue, my main destination.

Fun fact: The coffins appear small because they believe that when people die, they should go back in their form when they were in their mother's womb.

From Sagada, ride a jeepney to Bontoc (50 php, about 30 mins) then from Bontoc ride a UV (120 php, about 2 hrs) to Banaue. Bontoc is more of a civilization within a mountain in my perception.

I arrived at Banaue around 2:30 p.m. to find out that I missed the jeepney going to Batad (where I was going to stay) which left around 2:00 p.m.

Sighs.

So I was forced, yes forced, to ride the one-way tricycle worth 700 php. Pero natawaran ko guys naging 500 php. Pero, what is 150 php sa jeepney sa tricycle na 500 php di ba? :(

Anyway, I enjoyed every moment. I could not get my eyes off the scenery. It was breathtaking. We made a few stopovers just so I can take pictures of the view.

And, there we finally arrived at the edge of the mountain. Yes, you read that right, the EDGE of the mountain. I don't know how also, I got a bit worried seeing where kuya dropped me off. I have no idea where it was heading. But, I just trusted the Lord and took a 15-min walk down as instructed, carrying 3 bags watching over how I can fall off if I make one wrong step. I got tired and anxious walking alone then I heard two kids laughing behind me.

I kept walking and looked.

Scared actually. 

Tapos, pagtingin ko sa dinaanan ko one foot away na ko sa paglaglag ko.

I got ashamed of myself for thinking badly about what the people would do to me and saw those two kids with hollow-blocks on their backs. I followed them and could not believe my eyes for what I witnessed. My heart dropped and all the tiredness went away in an instant.

There was a village, right down the mountain.

It was not any other place like I have been to.

It was so peaceful.

Mothers were having the best time of their lives. May paso sa ulo nila tapos nagpapaunahan sila. Kids were playing on the corner of the elementary school and every person just smiled at me and muntik na ko maiyak.

You may be feeling afraid right now, or fear is holding you back, or you maybe in a point wherein you have no idea where you are heading. But, God's got you. He'll keep you safe and sure.

-End-

CHAPTER III

I went on to find Ramon's Native Homestay and Restaurant. I asked a lot of people and grabe pala, nasa baba ng bundok. I just saw it on facebook. They are very accommodating and the place had a great view. The restroom is clean. The rooms available range from 250-500 php. They give free tea and coffee, too. If I will rate the place I will give it a 5 out of 5.

Warning though: Nasa baba po ito ng bundok. Three times Pasig Science 6th floor stairs friends with no handrails and very very steep.

Hingal na hingal ako pagbaba honestly. But when I got there, I never wanted to go back up anymore, literally and figuratively.

Photo: Batad Rice Terraces, Ifugao, Mountain Province

Guess who slept for more than 12 hours straight? From 4:30 p.m. to around 5:30 a.m. the next day. I wanted to see the sunrise so I immediately like rushed off to the mountains and went up.

I did not mention about eating.

That's because I did not. For some reason, nakalimutan kong di pa ko kumakain. I did not feel hungry din though.

After all the excitement, my sugar level dropped. My knees were trembling. I felt really dizzy. I was hungry and I was nearly on top so I continued going up. Until, my vision actually blacked-out. This was something I did not expect that would happen.

I laid here for quite some time,

Photo: Taken an hour after

My whole body was shaking. I was catching my breath and I had no one around me. It's funny how one small decision could put your life at stake. Please, don't forget to eat. Eat on time. Eat like your life depends on it. :(

I was devastated. I am stuck in a situation I did not know how to get out of at that very moment.

But, something happens when you look up.

Right where I was lying down, I saw "Samcy's Grocery" and oh my gosh food at last. Pero nasa taas nanaman ng bundok wala na ko energy finish na.

During my first few steps up, I shouted,

E: "Tao po?"

With my voice shaking yung tipong paiyak na guys, awang-awa ako sa sarili ko. All because hindi ako kumain. :(

Ate: "Ano po yon?"

E: "May kanin po kayo?" :'(

(Just imagine me sitting on the stairs, hinang hina na and my face facing down on my knees)

Ate: "Wala po, grocery po ito"

Pain in the heart.

But wait, there's more..

Ate: "Pero akyat na po kayo lutuan nalang po namin kayo"

God bless your kind soul ate talaga. 

Siguro naawa sakin si ate, kase ako din awang-awa na ko sa sarili ko.

I went up with the remaining energy that I had and she cooked noddles for me. :( She like literally saved me from my wrong decision. There I met, kuya Marcus, Bruce and Marjorie too! They are kindest, most thoughtful and loving people I have ever met in my life. If it was not for them I do not know anymore? Iyak nalang ako.

Here's the catch, while I was eating kuya Marcus played a worship song.

Goosebumps. 

I was reminded of how faithful God is that He led me to those people. I believe God was teaching me a valuable lesson about making decisions abruptly.

In life, as people we tend to rush into things. Do things impulsively and forget that Someone is reigning over our lives. Take it slow, let God lead the way.

After eating and listening to worship songs. I regained my energy and kuya Marcus toured me around the terraces for free. There, I experienced and saw with my very own eyes the beauty of God's creation and man's creation combined.

-End-

CHAPTER IV

Remember the jeepney that goes to Batad from Banaue at 2:00 p.m. It fetches people from Batad at 9:00 a.m.

I went back to my inn, took a shower and prepared my things.

Yes,

Aakyat ako ulit guys dun sa bundok at dadaan dun sa makitid na daan patungo sa kabihasnan :(

It was already 8:45 a.m. if I miss the jeepney, I will ride the one-way tricycle again.

Dead tired. I stopped around 5-6 times during the climb up the mountain and I stopped 3-5 times as I was walking through my way back to where I was dropped off yesterday.

I lost hope. I knew I was not going to make it in time. So I rested.

While I was resting I saw some students passing right at me and I thought, wow these children do this everyday just to get to school and I was there for just a day? Who am I to complain?

One girl stopped right in front at me and smiled,

I got back up and kept on walking. I stopped and walked, stopped, walked. I felt like it was the longest walk of my life. It was easier going down but going up? I was on the verge of giving up.

Until,

Kuya: "Ma'am Banaue po?"

E: "Opo"

Kuya: "May tricycle po kayo?"

E: "Wala po, nandon pa po ba yung jeep?"

Kuya: "Ako po yung driver" :(

Goosebumps. 

Wait, di pa tapos..

Kuya: "May nakakita po kase sainyo na mga estudyante di na daw po kayo makalakad kaya po sinundo ko na po kayo dito."

Goosebumps ulit.

And, I was crying behind him. Thank You Lord, I knew this was You. Thank you rin kuya. Thank you dun sa students. This was the third time someone saved me from the pain and struggle that I was going through. These people do not even know who I am but their hearts are just the purest and words will never suffice of how great they are.

When I rode the jeepney, everyone followed as if they waited for me, and wala akong masabi kundi thank you nalang.

I was looking for some of the boys na nakita ko din and I could not find them.

I went out and saw them on top of the jeep!

E: "Pwede ako dyan?"

Kuya: "Pwede po"

Ayun, I witnessed the whole beauty of Batad and its people in just a small amount of time that I stayed there. I overheard them talking about how lucky they were that there was a jeepney available. So I asked,

E: "Paano kung walang jeep?"

Kuya: "Naglalakad po kami"

Shookt. 

E: "Gaano katagal lakad niyo?"

Kuya: "3 hours"

I was not able to utter another word after that. When we got to their school he said,

Kuya: "Ate ayan po yung paaralan namin"

I never thought that there could be an entirely different world from mine. I complain about too many things. Rant about the silliest things and long for more than what I have. When others, simply desire to have a jeepney ride.

Complaining about being stuck in traffic for 3 hours? Compared to walking to school for 3 hours? Complaining about carrying bags with clothes in it when children carry hollow-blocks for a living. Complaining about slow internet when others barely have mobile signal to communicate with their loved ones.

All of these things. Thought me how to be grateful. To stop being a prejudice. To be contented and to appreciate more. Through this journey, I fell more deeply in love with the Lord through every smiling face, through the care and love of the Ifugao natives, through His creation and more importantly, His presence.

I may have gone through a lot of trials but one thing is for sure, I was never alone.

-End-
